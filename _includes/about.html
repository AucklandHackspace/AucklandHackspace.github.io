INFO

Hackland is an independent, not for profit Hackspace in central Auckland for Ukelele, Coffee, Ethereum, Bitcoin, 3D Printing, CNC, Woodworking, Metalworking, Electronics, Coding, Sewing, Arts and Crafts or just hanging out with creative people. You don't need to have a project or know how to use any tools or know what you are doing - most people just hang out

Guiding Principles

Change and Experimentation is welcome, even if you're not an expert or not sure where something is going. Everything in the space is hackable unless it has a "Do Not Hack" sticker on it. If it looks expensive or hacking it would take longer than 6 hours to put back, please check with the group or post on the Slack channel or Loomio before taking it to bits.

Big Changes & Group Consensus: if a change is big, talk about it with as many people as possible in person, then ask on slack and post a Loomio proposal to see how the wider-group feels about it. The aim is always to reach a compromise everyone is okay with, but one which is best for the entire group, not one individual.

Projects with Deadlines: the space is mostly incompatible with those who need to quickly build a one off thing which has deadlines - there are plenty of commercial spaces which are set up for this. This includes construction of camper vans, particularly if you are unemployed in New Zealand and a non-resident. Overuse of equiptment and leaving of left over material and rubbish has unfortunately required us to enforce this policy. Please check out Valhalla Acoomodation as an alternative.

Safety: there are dangerous tools and equipment in the space. Please have someone show you how to safely use any tool you are unfamilar with before turning them on. Always use the correct safety equipment protect other members from exposure to dangerous things and always warn other memberes in the space before making loud noises.

Materials and Donations: High quality materials are welcomed at Hackland. We cannot accept everything as the space becomes too crowded to be useable. Every now and then we need to tidy up and through out old junk which costs us money to dispose of. Please don't donate broken electronic equiptment, please take it to a specialist e-waste recycler such as: https://www.computerrecycling.co.nz/ and dispose of it responsibly. MDF is not permited in hackland as the dust is very dangerous to humans.
